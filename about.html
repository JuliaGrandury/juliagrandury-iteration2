About Me

As a child, I was known to never sit still. I always had to explore what was behind a door, test the strength of a barrier, or climb to the top of a tree to see past what everybody else saw. I never accepted things to be one dimensional and felt the need to explore every aspect of them. This curiosity and desire to understand have remained fundamental to the person I am and seek to become.

Interests

Dancing I participated in a pre-professional dance program at the School at Steps for 10 years and have continued to pursue my passion since. I enjoy all forms including ballet, modern, jazz, musical theater and salsa.

Windsurfing I first started windsurfing in 2018 at the renown French sailing school and non-profit organization Les Glénans. I have since become a certified windsurfing instructor and am training to become a catamaran and

Hiking & Trekking In 2017, I discovered a new passion of mine on my first camping trip. Since then I have hiked parts of the Appalachian Trail, the Camino de Compostel and in 2019, my sister and I climbed Mount Kilimanjaro for which you can check out my blog here.

Climbing I am top rope certified and love to climb and boulder both outdoors and indoors in my free time. (Yes, I usually wear shoes when I do!)

Traveling I have traveled around the world with family, friends and alone. So far, I have been to 32 countries and 5 continents. My next trips in the works are Nepal and New Zealand.

Music I pride myself on my 70,000+ spotify minutes and love to create playlists for my friends and family. I have always been drawn to music even though it was not very present in the environment I grew up in. I love seeeing live music in my free time and a couple of years ago, I decided to start teaching myself to play the ukulele and guitar.

Volunteering

South By Southwest Conference Volunteer
I volunteered as Stage Crew for the South by Southwest concerts between 03/14/22 and 03/20/22. I facilitated the stage setup and production while also accomodating for the needs of artists. This enabled me to attend events during the rest of the week such as SXSW talks and panels.

Election Poll Worker
Until the recent elections, most poll workers had typically been over the age of 60. As they were more vulnerable to COVID-19, few were comfortable with working the polls during the pandemic. Thus, in November of 2020, the nation faced a shortage of poll workers leaving some polling locations at risk of being understaffed or unable to open. This motivated me to volunteer as a poll worker for the presidential elections.

Be My Eyes Volunteer
Be My Eyes is a free tool for blind and visually impaired individuals to connect with volunteers that will "lend their eyesight" and support them through everyday tasks. Thanks to over 5 million volunteers, users can get help with small daily tasks such as reading small print, troubleshooting technology and so much more.
